NEIGHBORS

Dan and Meagan met at the University of Vermont’s Honors College Dormitory, where they were conveniently assigned as neighbors. At first, Meagan was a little thrown off by Dan’s sarcasm and whit, but they quickly connected and became close friends. That first year consisted of many late night Mario-Kart and Guitar Hero battles, as well as intense philosophical discussions. Movie nights and binge watching television shows also became a staple- highlights include Dan calling WALL-E “the cutest thing [he’s] ever seen.”

ROOMIES

By the end of their freshman year, Dan and Meagan had cemented their friendship (despite the fact he forgot Meagan’s Birthday…) and decided to become roommates sophomore year. Throughout fall of 2009, they became best friends and it was clear that this was not going to be an ordinary friendship.

AND MORE

In the beginning of 2010, Dan finally confessed his feelings were, in fact, more. Their first date was seeing “Up in the Air,” followed by dinner at Three Tomatoes, on Valentine’s Day. While Dan had met Meagan’s parents previously, he got a crash course in DiVito family dynamics when he went to New Hampshire for Easter. Thankfully, nothing significant happened.

However, when Meagan met Dan’s extended family for the first time, Uncle Bob mistook the back of Meagan’s head for Dan’s sister Kate- and promptly gave Meagan a noogie. Needless to say, Bob was as surprised as Meagan. Dan and Meagan spent the summer traveling back and forth between MA and NH, and were very happy to return to UVM as RAs in Fall 2010.

NEW ROOM WITH A VIEW AND A FURRY FRIEND

Dan and Meagan moved in together June, 2011 on a horse farm in Colchester, VT. Mismatched furniture galore, their first home was the epitome of college living. Because they had spent much of the past 3 years living in the same area, this transition was not a big as it would seem. The hardest transition of all was Dan learning how to cook.

The couple decided they were ready for a new addition to the household and found a lop eared rabbit to complete their hearts and home. Chewy quickly adapted to his new owners and while he still won’t cuddle, he loves his head scratched and lounging in the bathroom.

close

GRADUATION AND A BIG MOVE

Their time at UVM came to an end May of 2012, which meant big changes for the couple. They both accepted summer jobs at the YMCA, where they were conveniently stationed next to each other the entire summer. Meagan was offered an internship with the National Institutes of Health in Washington, DC, which allowed her to become a Registered Dietitian, while Dan was offered an internship at the office of Senator Scott Brown.

Dan was eventually offered a position at the National Regulatory Research Institute as a Research Assistant. With financial reassurance finally in hand, they were able to branch out into the DC area, falling in love with the culture and foodie scene.

HE PUT A RING ON IT

2013 was full of excitement for the couple, as Meagan completed her program and was offered a contractor position at the NIH. Dan was also promoted and started traveling, writing independent papers and giving presentations to regulators across the country.

The couple took a family trip to London in the fall, where it was speculated a shiny object may present itself. Dan proposed to Meagan after ice-skating at the National Science Museum in London. While he didn’t plan on it occurring there (or dropping the ring in front of Meagan at Kensington Palace), the lighted trees and beautiful scenery made it a magical moment.

close
more

PLANNING AND A PUPPY

2014 has been full of changes for the couple. In April, they moved into a 1 bedroom townhome and Meagan started her new job at Kaiser Permanente. In September, they adopted their Border Collie rescue, Ellie, who has provided a new energy to their relationship.

2014 has also been full of wedding planning. While they thought 2 years would be needed to plan a long distance wedding, they quickly found it wasn’t. But it has given them a lot of time to thoroughly plan their honeymoon to Italy!

MR. AND MRS. PHELAN

On September 13, 2015, Dan and Meagan will become husband and wife in front of their closest family and friends. They are planning a small ceremony on Sunset Lake in Alton, NH, followed by a larger reception at The Inn on Main in Wolfeboro, NH. They are very excited to share this day with their family and friends!
